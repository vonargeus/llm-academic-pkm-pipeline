# Summary

The paper introduces [[SimulSeamless]], a system developed by FBK for the [[IWSLT 2024]] [[Simultaneous Speech Translation]] task. The authors solve the problem of adapting large offline [[Foundation Models]] for real-time tasks by combining the [[SeamlessM4T]] model with the [[AlignAtt]] inference policy. Their main finding is that this "off-the-shelf" approach, which requires no retraining or task-specific adaptation, achieves competitive or superior results compared to specialized systems across multiple language pairs.

# Main Contribution

- Proposed [[SimulSeamless]], a framework that integrates the [[SeamlessM4T]] foundation model with the [[AlignAtt]] simultaneous policy.
- Demonstrated that offline-trained [[Speech-to-Text Translation]] models can be effectively repurposed for simultaneous inference without expensive retraining.
- Achieved state-of-the-art or competitive performance on the [[IWSLT 2024 Shared Task]] for English-German, English-Japanese, English-Chinese, and Czech-English.
- Released an open-source implementation that supports simultaneous translation for over 143 source and 200 target languages.

# Research Problem

The research focuses on the challenge of adapting large-scale offline [[Speech-to-Text Translation]] models for [[Simultaneous Speech Translation]] (SimulST). Traditional SimulST systems often require specialized training to manage the [[quality-latency tradeoff]], which is computationally expensive and difficult to scale for [[Foundation Models]]. This paper addresses the gap in the literature regarding how to leverage massive, pre-trained multimodal models like [[SeamlessM4T]] for real-time applications without the need for ad-hoc fine-tuning or architectural modifications.

# Method

The authors present [[SimulSeamless]], which utilizes the [[SeamlessM4T]] (medium configuration) as the underlying engine. [[SeamlessM4T]] consists of a [[W2V-BERT 2.0]] encoder for [[self-supervised speech representations]], a [[Length Adapter]] based on the [[M-adaptor]] for [[attention pooling]], and an [[NLLB]] decoder. 

To enable simultaneous capabilities, they apply the [[AlignAtt]] policy. [[AlignAtt]] uses [[cross-attention]] scores from the decoder to determine if the model has sufficient information to emit a token. It assigns predicted words to specific audio frames; if a word aligns with the most recently received frames, the system waits for more input to ensure stability. This differs from [[SeamlessStreaming]], which relies on [[EMMA]] and requires significant retraining. The authors also implemented frame-wise normalization of [[cross-attention]] scores to prevent weights from being skewed toward specific frame representations.

# Dataset / Experimental Setup

The system was evaluated on the [[IWSLT 2024 Shared Task]] benchmarks. For English to German, Japanese, and Chinese, the authors used [[MuST-C]] v2.0 (tst-COMMON). For Czech to English, they used the official IWSLT 2024 dev set. The experimental setup utilized the [[SeamlessM4T]]-medium checkpoint (1.2B parameters) on a single [[NVIDIA V100]] GPU. Audio was processed in incremental chunks: 1s for En-De and Cs-En, 800ms for En-Zh, and 400ms for En-Ja.

# Evaluation Metrics

- [[BLEU]]: Used to measure translation quality via [[sacreBLEU]].
- [[Average Lagging]] (AL): The primary metric for measuring the delay between speech and text emission.
- [[Length Adaptive Average Lagging]] (LAAL) and [[Average Token Delay]] (ATD): Secondary latency metrics used for the official task scoring.
- [[quality-latency tradeoff]]: Analyzed to determine the optimal balance for different application domains.

# Key Results

- [[SimulSeamless]] achieved [[BLEU]] scores of approximately 27-28 for English-German at an [[Average Lagging]] of ~2 seconds.
- The system outperformed or matched many of the previous year's submissions that used task-specific training.
- Layer-wise analysis revealed that extracting [[cross-attention]] scores from specific decoder layers (e.g., layer 27 or 28 for En-De) is more effective than averaging across all layers.
- The approach proved robust across diverse language pairs, including those with significant structural differences like English-Japanese and English-Chinese.

# Limitations

The performance of the system is highly sensitive to the choice of the decoder layer used for [[cross-attention]] extraction, which requires manual tuning for different language pairs. Additionally, while the method avoids retraining, the 1.2B parameter size of [[SeamlessM4T]] presents a high computational overhead for real-time inference on low-resource hardware. The authors also noted that [[cross-attention]] scores can sometimes be skewed, requiring normalization to maintain policy reliability.
