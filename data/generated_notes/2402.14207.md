# Summary

This paper introduces [[STORM]], a system designed to assist [[Large Language Models]] in writing grounded, long-form, Wikipedia-like articles from scratch. The authors identify that the primary bottleneck in [[Long-form article generation]] is the [[Pre-writing stage]], which involves extensive research and planning. [[STORM]] solves this by simulating a multi-perspective conversation where agents ask diverse questions to a grounded expert to discover information and synthesize a comprehensive [[Outline]]. The study finds that [[STORM]] significantly improves the breadth and organization of generated articles compared to standard [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation]] baselines.

# Main Contribution

- Proposed the [[STORM]] paradigm, which automates the [[Pre-writing stage]] through [[Multi-perspective question asking]] and [[Conversational simulation]].
- Developed a novel approach for [[Perspective discovery]] by analyzing existing [[Wikipedia]] structures of similar topics to guide information gathering.
- Curated [[FreshWiki]], a dataset of high-quality, recent [[Wikipedia]] articles to evaluate generation without the risk of training data leakage.
- Established a framework for [[Outline assessments]] to quantitatively measure the quality of the research and planning phase in [[Expository Writing]].

# Research Problem

The paper addresses the difficulty [[Large Language Models]] face when generating long-form, factual content that requires both high-level organization and deep topical research. While [[LLM]]s excel at short-form text, they often suffer from [[Hallucination]] or lack of detail when dealing with [[Long-tail topics]] using only [[Parametric knowledge]]. Existing [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation]] (RAG) methods often bypass the critical [[Pre-writing stage]]—the process of identifying what information is needed and how to structure it—which is essential for creating high-quality, [[Wikipedia]]-style articles.

# Method

The [[STORM]] method consists of two main stages: the [[Pre-writing stage]] and the writing stage. In the pre-writing stage, the system first performs [[Perspective discovery]] by retrieving existing [[Wikipedia]] outlines of similar topics to identify diverse viewpoints (e.g., an "event planner" perspective for an Olympic ceremony topic). It then initiates [[Perspective-Guided Question Asking]], where the [[LLM]] adopts these personas to ask specific questions.

These questions are processed through [[Conversational Question Asking]], a simulated multi-turn dialogue between a "writer" agent and an "expert" agent. The expert agent is grounded in real-time [[Information Retrieval]] from the Internet. This iterative process allows the system to refine its understanding and uncover deeper facts. Finally, the collected information is synthesized into a structured [[Outline]]. In the writing stage, the system uses this outline to perform [[Outline-driven retrieval-augmented generation]], expanding each section into a full-length article with citations.

# Dataset / Experimental Setup

The authors curated the [[FreshWiki]] dataset, consisting of [[Wikipedia]] articles created or significantly edited between February 2022 and September 2023. This timeframe ensures that the content was likely not included in the training data of the [[LLM]]s being tested (e.g., GPT-3.5/4). The evaluation compared [[STORM]] against two baselines: **Direct Gen** (generating from parametric knowledge) and **Outline-driven RAG** (a standard RAG approach that generates an outline and searches for each section without the multi-perspective conversational research).

# Evaluation Metrics

- **[[Outline assessments]]**: Evaluated against human-written [[Wikipedia]] outlines using metrics for [[Breadth]] (coverage of essential topics) and [[Organization]] (logical structure).
- **Expert evaluation**: Experienced [[Wikipedia]] editors reviewed the articles for factual accuracy, neutrality, and depth.
- **Automatic Metrics**: Used to measure the alignment between the generated content and the reference [[FreshWiki]] articles.

# Key Results

- [[STORM]] articles were judged to be significantly more organized than the baseline, showing a **25% absolute increase** in organization scores.
- The coverage of topics improved by **10%** in terms of [[Breadth]] compared to standard [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation]].
- Expert editors preferred [[STORM]]'s articles for their comprehensive nature, noting that the [[Multi-perspective question asking]] successfully surfaced details that simpler search strategies missed.
- The system proved particularly effective for [[Long-tail topics]] where the model's internal knowledge was insufficient.

# Limitations

The authors note that [[STORM]] is susceptible to **source bias transfer**, where biases present in the retrieved Internet sources are reflected in the final article. Additionally, the system occasionally exhibits **over-association**, where the [[LLM]] fabricates connections between unrelated facts found during the research phase. There is also a risk of the model failing to resolve contradictions between different retrieved sources.
