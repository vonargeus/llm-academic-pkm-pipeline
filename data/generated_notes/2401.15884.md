# Summary

This paper addresses the problem of [[Large Language Models (LLMs)]] exhibiting [[hallucinations]] and the heavy reliance of [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)]] on the relevance of retrieved documents. The authors propose [[Corrective Retrieval Augmented Generation (CRAG)]], a method to improve RAG's robustness when initial retrieval fails. [[CRAG]] employs a [[lightweight retrieval evaluator]] to assess document quality, triggers [[large-scale web searches]] for correction, and utilizes a [[decompose-then-recompose algorithm]] to refine retrieved content. Experiments demonstrate that [[CRAG]] significantly enhances the performance of [[RAG-based approaches]] across both [[short-form generation]] and [[long-form generation]] tasks.

# Research Problem

[[Large Language Models (LLMs)]] frequently suffer from [[hallucinations]] and [[factual errors]] because the accuracy of their generated texts cannot be solely guaranteed by their internal [[parametric knowledge]]. While [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)]] offers a practical solution by incorporating external knowledge, its effectiveness is critically dependent on the relevance and accuracy of the retrieved documents. This raises a significant concern: how does the model perform when the retrieval process fails or returns irrelevant information, potentially leading to misleading outputs? This paper specifically addresses this gap by studying scenarios where the [[retriever]] yields inaccurate results and proposing strategies to enhance [[RAG]]'s robustness in such cases.

# Method

The proposed approach is [[Corrective Retrieval Augmented Generation (CRAG)]], a plug-and-play method designed to self-correct [[retriever]] results and improve the utilization of documents for augmenting generation. [[CRAG]] operates by first employing a [[lightweight retrieval evaluator]] to assess the overall quality of retrieved documents for a given query. This evaluator quantifies a [[confidence degree]] based on which different [[knowledge retrieval actions]]—{Correct, Incorrect, Ambiguous}—can be triggered.

For scenarios classified as 'Incorrect' or 'Ambiguous', [[CRAG]] integrates [[large-scale web searches]] as a strategic extension. This is crucial because retrieval from static and limited corpora can often yield suboptimal documents in terms of scope and diversity. By harnessing the expansive and dynamic nature of the web, [[CRAG]] broadens the spectrum of retrieved information, complementing and enriching the initially obtained documents.

Furthermore, to eliminate redundant contexts within retrieved documents that are unhelpful for [[RAG]], a [[decompose-then-recompose algorithm]] is meticulously crafted. This algorithm refines the retrieved information, optimizing the extraction of key insights and minimizing the inclusion of non-essential elements, thereby enhancing the efficient utilization of retrieved data by the [[generator]]. [[CRAG]] is experimentally implemented into both standard [[RAG]] and [[Self-RAG]] to demonstrate its adaptability and effectiveness.

# Dataset / Experimental Setup

The experiments were conducted on four distinct datasets covering both [[short-form generation]] and [[long-form generation]] tasks.
