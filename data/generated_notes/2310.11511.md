# Summary

[[Large Language Models|LLMs]] often suffer from factual inaccuracies due to their reliance on [[parametric knowledge]], and traditional [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation|RAG]] methods retrieve passages indiscriminately, which can lead to low-quality generations. This paper introduces [[SELF-RAG]], a framework that trains a single [[Large Language Models|LM]] to adaptively retrieve passages [[on-demand retrieval|on-demand]] and self-reflect on its generations using special [[reflection tokens]]. These [[reflection tokens]] enable the [[Large Language Models|LM]] to critique its own output and select the best generation, enhancing quality and [[factuality]]. Experiments demonstrate that [[SELF-RAG]] significantly outperforms state-of-the-art [[Large Language Models|LLMs]] and [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation|RAG]] models across diverse tasks, showing substantial gains in [[factuality]] and [[citation accuracy]].

# Main Contribution

-   Trained a single [[Large Language Models|LM]] to adaptively retrieve passages and generate [[reflection tokens]] ([[retrieval tokens]] and [[critique tokens]]) to control behavior during inference.
-   Developed a customizable decoding algorithm using [[segment-level beam search]] based on [[reflection token]] probabilities for flexible and controlled generation.

# Research Problem

The core research problem addressed is the persistent issue of factual inaccuracies in [[Large Language Models|LLM]] outputs, primarily stemming from their sole reliance on [[parametric knowledge]]. While [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation|RAG]] approaches mitigate this by augmenting [[Large Language Models|LLMs]] with external knowledge, they often retrieve passages indiscriminately, regardless of necessity or relevance, which can introduce irrelevant information and diminish [[Large Language Models|LM]] versatility or lead to low-quality generations. Furthermore, conventional [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation|RAG]] does not guarantee consistency between the output and retrieved passages. This paper aims to bridge this gap by developing a method that allows [[Large Language Models|LLMs]] to intelligently decide when to retrieve information and to critically evaluate the quality and [[factuality]] of their own generations.

# Method

[[SELF-RAG]] (Self-Reflective Retrieval-Augmented Generation) is a novel framework that trains an arbitrary [[Large Language Models|LM]] to perform adaptive retrieval and self-reflection. Unlike traditional [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation|RAG]], [[SELF-RAG]] integrates special [[reflection tokens]] into the generation process, which are categorized into [[retrieval tokens]] and [[critique tokens]]. These tokens allow the [[Large Language Models|LM]] to control its behavior during inference, enabling it to tailor its generation to specific task requirements and improve output quality.

The [[SELF-RAG]] process involves three key steps during generation. First, given an input prompt and preceding generations, the [[Large Language Models|LM]] determines if augmenting the continued generation with retrieved passages would be helpful. If so, it outputs a [[retrieval tokens|retrieval token]] that triggers an [[on-demand retrieval]] from a retriever model. Second, [[SELF-RAG]] concurrently processes multiple retrieved passages, evaluating their relevance, and then generates corresponding task outputs. Finally, it generates [[critique tokens]] to self-criticize its own output segments, assessing aspects like [[factuality]] and overall quality, and then selects the best segment. This iterative process allows for fine-grained control and self-correction, distinguishing it from conventional [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation|RAG]] which retrieves a fixed number of documents without self-assessment.

The [[SELF-RAG]] generator [[Large Language Models|LM]] is trained end-to-end to generate text interleaved with these [[reflection tokens]] and retrieved passages. The [[reflection tokens]] are inserted offline into the training corpus by a pre-trained [[critic model]], which is partly supervised on a dataset of input, output, and corresponding [[reflection tokens]] collected by prompting a proprietary [[Large Language Models|LM]] like [[GPT-4]]. This offline insertion reduces the overhead during training. Furthermore, [[SELF-RAG]] employs a customizable decoding algorithm that leverages [[reflection token]] predictions through [[segment-level beam search]]. This allows for flexible adjustment of retrieval frequency and customization of model behaviors based on user preferences or task constraints, by weighting the linear sum of [[reflection token]] probabilities as a segment score.

# Dataset / Experimental Setup

The [[SELF-RAG]] model was trained on a diverse collection of text interleaved with [[reflection tokens]] and retrieved passages. The [[critic model]] used to insert [[reflection tokens]] was supervised on a dataset of input, output, and corresponding [[reflection tokens]] collected by prompting [[GPT-4]].

For evaluation, [[SELF-RAG]] was tested on a diverse set of six tasks, including [[Open-domain QA]], [[Fact verification]], and [[Reasoning]] tasks, as well as long-form generation. The experiments compared [[SELF-RAG]] (7B and 13B parameters) against several state-of-the-art [[Large Language Models|LLMs]] and [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation|RAG]] approaches, including ChatGPT, retrieval-augmented Llama2-chat, and Alpaca.

# Evaluation Metrics

-   [[factuality]]: To assess the correctness and truthfulness of the generated information.
-   [[citation accuracy]]: To measure how well the generated output is supported by and consistent with the retrieved passages, especially for long-form generations.

# Key Results

-   [[SELF-RAG]] (7B and 13B parameters) significantly outperformed state-of-the-art [[Large Language Models|LLMs]] and retrieval-augmented models on a diverse set of tasks.
-   Specifically, [[SELF-RAG]] outperformed ChatGPT and retrieval-augmented Llama2-chat on [[Open-domain QA]], [[Reasoning]], and [[Fact verification]] tasks.
-   It showed significant gains in improving [[factuality]] and [[citation accuracy]] for long-form generations relative to these baseline models.
-   [[SELF-RAG]] also outperformed Llama2-chat and Alpaca on all evaluated tasks.
-   Analysis demonstrated the effectiveness of training and inference with [[reflection tokens]] for overall performance improvements and enabling test-time model customizations, such as balancing the trade-off between [[citation accuracy]] and completeness.

# Limitations

While the paper presents strong results, it does not explicitly detail limitations of [[SELF-RAG]]. Potential limitations could include the computational overhead associated with the [[segment-level beam search]] decoding algorithm and processing multiple passages in parallel during inference, despite the claim of reduced training overhead. Additionally, the reliance on a [[critic model]] trained with [[GPT-4]] data for generating [[reflection tokens]] might introduce biases or limitations inherent to the proprietary [[Large Language Models|LM]] used.
