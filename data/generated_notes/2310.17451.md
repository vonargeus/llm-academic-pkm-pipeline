# Summary

This paper introduces [[Abductive visual Generation (AbdGen)]], an approach to integrate [[neural visual generative models]] with [[logical reasoning systems]] to enhance controllability, faithfulness, transparency, and generalizability. The authors propose a [[vector-quantized symbol grounding mechanism]] and a [[disentanglement training method]] to improve the control of logical symbols over generation. Furthermore, two [[logical abduction methods]] are developed to enable learning from few labeled data and to support the induction of [[latent logical generative rules]]. Experiments demonstrate that [[AbdGen]] can be integrated with various neural generative models, either by learning from scratch or utilizing pre-trained models, providing a systematic solution for [[logic-integrated neural visual generation]].

# Research Problem

This paper addresses the fundamental research problem of improving the controllability of [[neural visual generative models]] under external control signals, specifically by integrating them with [[logical reasoning systems]] to ensure faithfulness, transparency, and generalizability. While existing [[conditional generation]] techniques offer some control, they struggle to enforce rigorous logical regularities in generated outputs, leading to issues with strict logical verification, explainability, and generalization to significantly different semantics. This gap highlights the need for models that can leverage the strengths of both neural generation and symbolic reasoning, moving beyond the limitations of purely neural approaches or prior [[neurosymbolic]] attempts like [[VAEL]] which lacked mechanisms for few-shot learning and rule induction.

# Method

The proposed [[Abductive visual Generation (AbdGen)]] approach constructs a [[logic-integrated neural visual generative model]] by combining a [[neural visual generator]] with a [[logical reasoning system]]. The neural generator comprises a [[symbolic encoder]] (Esym), a [[sub-symbolic encoder]] (Esub), a [[vector-quantized grounding module]] (V), and a [[decoder]] (G). Esym extracts ungrounded latent embedding `zsym` (symbolic information), while Esub extracts symbol-independent visual features as [[sub-symbolic embedding]] `zsub` from a context image `x`. The core [[vector-quantized grounding module]] V maps `zsym` into [[grounded embeddings]] `zG` and their corresponding [[grounding symbol values]] `YG`, and can also retrieve [[grounded embeddings]] `zL` from [[logic-inferred symbol values]] `YL`. The decoder G then generates target images `x_hat` from `zL` and `zsub`.

A critical technical aspect is [[symbol grounding learning]], which involves training the neural symbol grounding module using an [[adversarial training]] method. This aims to achieve robust [[disentanglement training]] between symbolic and subsymbolic neural embeddings, thereby improving the controllability of logical symbols over the generation process, a key improvement over prior work like [[VAEL]].

Furthermore, [[AbdGen]] introduces two novel [[symbol grounding learning]] strategies based on the [[abductive learning]] framework to address challenges in learning from few labeled data and inducing [[latent logical generative rules]]. For scenarios without rule induction, an [[attention-based symbol verification mechanism]] is proposed to boost the efficiency of [[symbol grounding learning]]. In cases requiring rule induction, the method of [[contrastive meta-abduction]] is employed to conduct precise [[logical rule induction]] in conjunction with the [[symbol grounding learning]] process. This [[abductive learning]] approach offers a more direct search over the [[logic program synthesis]] space compared to [[DeepProblog]]'s reliance on [[possible world probabilities]], making it more efficient for learning from limited data.

# Limitations

The paper implicitly addresses limitations of prior work, such as [[VAEL]]'s assumption of sufficient instance-level labels for [[symbol grounding learning]] and its lack of mechanisms for [[rule induction]]. The authors also note that the complexity of [[latent presentations]] in [[neural visual generators]] and the intricate nature of [[generative process rules]] make the time cost of [[abduction]] a significant issue, necessitating new abduction strategies. While [[AbdGen]] aims to overcome these, the inherent complexity of integrating neural and symbolic systems, especially in ensuring perfect [[disentanglement training]] and efficient [[logic program synthesis]] for complex visual tasks, remains a challenge.
