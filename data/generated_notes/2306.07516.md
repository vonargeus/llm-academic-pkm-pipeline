# Summary

This paper addresses the challenging problem of determining the [[weight hierarchy]] and [[weight distribution]] of [[linear code]]s. The authors construct a new family of 3-weight $p$-ary linear codes using an extended [[defining-set construction]] based on two $p$-ary [[quadratic form]]s over different [[finite field]]s. By analyzing the properties of these quadratic forms and their character sums, they completely determine the weight distributions and the exact [[generalized Hamming weight]]s of the constructed codes. Furthermore, they show that most of these codes are minimal, making them highly useful for applications in [[secret sharing scheme]]s.

# Main Contribution

- Constructed a new family of 3-weight $p$-ary [[linear code]]s using two $p$-ary [[quadratic form]]s.
- Completely determined the [[weight distribution]]s and the exact [[weight hierarchy]] of the constructed codes.
- Demonstrated that most of the constructed codes are minimal and can be effectively applied to [[secret sharing scheme]]s.

# Research Problem

Determining the [[weight hierarchy]] (specifically the [[generalized Hamming weight]]s) of [[linear code]]s is a fundamental and notoriously difficult problem in [[Coding Theory]]. While the standard [[weight distribution]] provides the frequency of codeword weights, the weight hierarchy yields deeper structural information essential for analyzing the performance of codes in cryptographic settings, such as wiretap channels of type II and [[secret sharing scheme]]s. Prior literature has primarily focused on codes constructed from a single quadratic form or simpler linear functions. This paper addresses the gap of analyzing the complex interactions and weight structures of codes defined by the combination of two distinct $p$-ary [[quadratic form]]s over different [[finite field]]s.

# Method

The authors utilize an extended version of the [[defining-set construction]] originally proposed by Ding et al. They define a $p$-ary [[linear code]] $C_D$ of length $n$ over the [[finite field]] $\mathbb{F}_p$ using a defining set $D \subseteq \mathbb{F}_{q_1} \times \mathbb{F}_{q_2} \setminus \{(0,0)\}$ defined by the zero-set of the sum of two quadratic forms: 

$$D = \{(x, y) \in \mathbb{F}_{q_1} \times \mathbb{F}_{q_2} \setminus \{(0,0)\} : f(x) + g(y) = 0\}$$

where $f(x)$ and $g(y)$ are $p$-ary [[quadratic form]]s over $\mathbb{F}_{q_1}$ and $\mathbb{F}_{q_2}$ respectively. The codewords are generated using the [[trace function]]s over the respective fields.

To compute the [[generalized Hamming weight]]s $d_r(C_D)$, the authors translate the problem into finding the maximum intersection of the defining set $D$ with any $(s-r)$-dimensional subspace $H$ of the ambient space. This is achieved by restricting the quadratic forms to these subspaces and analyzing their ranks and signs. The authors employ advanced techniques in finite fields, including character sums, Weil sums, and the properties of the radical/kernel of the associated linearized polynomials, to systematically evaluate these intersections and derive the exact weight hierarchies.

# Dataset / Experimental Setup

As a purely theoretical paper in mathematical [[Coding Theory]], no physical datasets or empirical computer simulations were used. All results and proofs are derived analytically using algebraic methods, finite field arithmetic, and character theory.

# Evaluation Metrics

- [[generalized Hamming weight]] (GHW): Used to evaluate the cryptographic capabilities of the code, particularly in wiretap channels.
- [[weight distribution]]: Used to verify the 3-weight property and calculate the exact frequencies of the codeword weights.
- [[weight hierarchy]]: The complete sequence of GHWs $\{d_1, d_2, \dots, d_s\}$, providing a comprehensive structural profile of the code.
- [[minimum distance]]: Evaluated as the first GHW ($d_1$) to determine the error-correction capability of the code.

# Key Results

- Successfully constructed a class of $p$-ary [[linear code]]s with at most 3 non-zero weights.
- Completely determined the code length $n$: $n = p^{s-1} - 1$ when the sum of the ranks $R = R_f + R_g$ is odd, and $n = p^{s-1} - 1 + (p-1)p^{s-1}\epsilon_f\epsilon_g(p^*)^{-R/2}$ when $R$ is even.
- Derived explicit, closed-form expressions for the complete [[weight hierarchy]] $\{d_1, d_2, \dots, d_s\}$ of the constructed codes.
- Proved that the constructed codes are minimal under most parameter conditions, meaning they can be used to design [[secret sharing scheme]]s with highly desirable access structures.

# Limitations

- The construction and proofs are strictly limited to odd primes $p$, meaning the results do not directly apply to binary linear codes ($p=2$).
- The methodology heavily relies on the specific algebraic properties of [[quadratic form]]s, making it difficult to generalize to higher-degree algebraic functions or arbitrary non-linear mappings.
